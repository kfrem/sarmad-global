Delivering independent statutory audits, voluntary audits, and specialized assurance reviews. Regulated as registered auditors by both the ACCA and ICAEW.

Rigorous Audit Quality and Assurance Standards

An audit is not merely a statutory obligation; it represents a powerful mechanism to ensure financial integrity, verify internal controls, and reassure lenders, shareholders, and institutional investors of your firm's stability. Our audit division provides methodical, objective testing of your financial statements, identifying operational vulnerabilities and structural opportunities for improvement.

We carry out both statutory audits for companies exceeding the audit exemption thresholds and voluntary audits for businesses looking to secure finance, prepare for sale, or strengthen corporate governance. Our audits are planned meticulously and executed under strict international standards on auditing (UK).

Complete Assurance and Audit Services

Our auditing and assurance scope includes:

  • ✔ Statutory Audits: Fulfilling Companies House audit criteria for large and mid-sized limited companies under SIC code 69201.
  • ✔ Voluntary Audits: Independent financial verification to support high-value bank lending, alternative funding, or investor reviews.
  • ✔ Specialist Assurance Reviews: Targeted financial verification, grant audits, and corporate transaction due diligence.
  • ✔ Internal Control Reviews: Systematic analysis of your operational systems, bookkeeping controls, and security processes to eliminate fraudulent risks.

Registered Auditors: Dual ACCA & ICAEW Accreditation

Audit & Assurance FAQs

Under current UK law, a limited company is generally exempt from a statutory audit if it meets at least two of the following criteria in a financial year: an annual turnover of no more than £10.2 million, a balance sheet total of no more than £5.1 million, or an average of 50 or fewer employees.

Many owner-managed businesses commission a voluntary audit to secure better rates on bank lending, satisfy supplier requirements, prepare for an eventual corporate sale, or simply reassure non-active shareholders of absolute financial accuracy.

We work collaboratively with your internal finance team, preparing a detailed audit requirements checklist, organizing transaction testing scopes in advance, and minimizing operational disruptions during the audit fieldwork.
