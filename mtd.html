Making Tax Digital for Income Tax

Clear MTD guidance, downloads and implementation support.

Use this page to understand what HMRC is changing, download simplified guides, and get help preparing clean digital records for quarterly reporting.

Download Free Guides View HMRC Links

Who needs to prepare?

  • 6 April 2026Sole traders and landlords with qualifying income over £50,000.
  • 6 April 2027Qualifying income over £30,000.
  • 6 April 2028Qualifying income over £20,000.
What changes

MTD is a reporting routine, not just a form.

HMRC is moving affected sole traders and landlords into digital record keeping, compatible software and quarterly updates. Quarterly updates are summaries of income and expenses, while the final tax return still deals with year-end adjustments and other income.

1Keep digital records

Income and expenses need to be captured in compatible software or controlled bridging records.

2Review quarterly

Records are checked, cleaned and summarised before each HMRC deadline.

3Submit and finalise

Quarterly updates are filed, then the final tax return is completed after year end.
